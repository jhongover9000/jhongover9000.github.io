me.

Korea, Japan, and the United States; I've lived about a third of my life in each of these countries and have picked up a large range of cultural values and experiences from them. Maybe as a result of this, my interests are just as diverse. Creative writing, coding, sports, theater, design, biology, and public speaking; these are just some of the many fields that I have found a place in. While this expansive net of interests and experiences may come off as indecisive, my intentions have always been the same: to learn, and to enjoy learning.

I'm told that I have a strange sense of humor and that something's not right with me, but I take that in stride because that's just who I am. I also like really minimalistic things, hence this website. Speaking of which...

this website.

This website might not really seem like much. And it's not. It's super minimalistic and is made up of more or less the essentials, and that's about it. But it's a design that I like a lot. It's also something that holds sentimental value.

You see, back when I was in high school and didn't know a single thing about coding or HTML, my friends and I decided that we wanted to create our own portfolio websites. Since we didn't know about coding, we used Google Sites (I know, look I knew literally nothing about coding then) to make the skeletons of our sites. But we never finished. Left incomplete, the website is somewhere out there in the Internet. And so I thought that this was a great opportunity to get some closure. I also know a lot more about coding and HTML, so I can make it how I envisioned it, too.

And so that's what this website is. Me completing what I started years ago.
